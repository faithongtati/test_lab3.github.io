Home > Traditions > Bavarian beer gardens

In a time when there was still no electrical refrigeration, the
brewers planted lime and chestnut trees over their beer cellars, and
spread a thick layer of gravel on the ground. This kept the cellars cool
and the beer fresh for longer. After buying their beer, customers were
happy to linger in the shade of the trees and enjoy a glass of “barley juice”.
This marked the birth of the genuine beer garden and the new decree allowed
the breweries to serve beer outside the inns for the first time.
