Home > Food > The best Bavarian beers to enjoy

Weihenstephan Hefe Weissbier
5.4%; £2.69 per 500ml
This clove-scented, golden blonde beer from Freising
near Munich impresses aficionados with its smooth, full-
bodied yeastiness and just-discernible hints of refreshing lemon.
Franziskaner Hefe Weissbier
5%; £1.80 per 500ml
This copper-coloured, mellow brew has quite a history,
with origins dating back to the oldest privately owned
brewery in Munich, founded in 1363. It’s naturally cloudy
and gently carbonated with a lush foam, and subtle notes of
banana can be detected as well as citrussy flavours.
Erdinger Weissbier
5.3%; £2.49 per 500 ml
The Erdinger Weissbräu is one of Germany’s largest
private breweries. It takes around three to four weeks to
mature and is ever so slightly malty and sweet, as well as
possessing a distinctive herbaceous note evoking freshly-cut grass.
